Skin Type and Skin Diseases

Actinic keratosis

Precancerous rough, scaly patches caused by prolonged exposure to sunlight.

Atopic Dermatitis

Chronic, itchy skin condition causing redness, dryness, and inflammation.

Benign Keratosis

Non-cancerous skin growth, often rough, brown, or wart-like in appearance.

Dermatofibroma

Firm, small, harmless skin nodule, usually on legs or arms.

Melanocytic nevus

Commonly known as a mole; pigmented, benign skin growth from melanocytes.

Melanoma

Serious skin cancer developing from moles; often dark, irregular, and fast-growing.

Squamous cell carcinoma

Common skin cancer; scaly, red patches or sores that may bleed.

Tinea Ringworm Candidiasis

Contagious ring-shaped rash caused by a skin-invading fungus.

Vascular lesion

Abnormal blood vessels appearing as red, purple, or blue marks.
